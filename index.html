Tongqing (Angelina) Chen

Ph.D. candidate, Operations Research/Operations Management · The University of Chicago Booth School of Business

Advisor: John R. Birge. I study statistical modeling, optimization, causal inference, reinforcement learning and general machine learning algorithms for data‑driven decision making. Broadly, I’m interested in turning noisy, high‑dimensional data into reliable decisions under constraints and uncertainty.

My work draws on convex and dynamic optimization, stochastic processes, and modern ML (causal inference, estimation, and learning‑to‑decide). I like problems that require both theory and scalable implementation—designing models with guarantees and validating them with large datasets.

Applications include healthcare (treatment‑effect estimation with partially observed confounders) and revenue management (dynamic pricing with network effects). Previously, I worked on promotion strategy analytics and user‑incentive targeting during internships at Uber.
